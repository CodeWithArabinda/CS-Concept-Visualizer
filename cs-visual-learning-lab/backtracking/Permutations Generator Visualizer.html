Algorithm Theory

Recursive Swapping

Permutations are generated by fixing an element at the current index (depth) and recursively permuting the remaining elements.

The Backtracking Step

To ensure we don't permanently modify the array, we backtrack by swapping the elements back to their original positions after returning from the recursive call.

Time Complexity O(N × N!)
Space Complexity O(N)
