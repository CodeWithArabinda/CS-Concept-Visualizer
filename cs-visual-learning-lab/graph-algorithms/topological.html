Kahn's Algorithm

Topological Sorting provides a linear ordering of vertices such that for every directed edge U → V, vertex U comes before V in the ordering.

Process: It repeatedly finds a node with zero incoming edges (in-degree = 0), "removes" it from the graph, and updates the incoming edges of its neighbors until all nodes are processed.
